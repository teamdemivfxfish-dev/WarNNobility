War ’n Nobility

a living ladder of rank, seals and vassalage for the MineColonies world
NeoForge 1.21.1  |  modid warnnobility  |  v1.0.0  |  by NewtL
Alpha requires MineColonies + War ’n Taxes + Easy Factions + Antique Atlas 4

War ’n Nobility lays a CK-style ladder of noble rank over your server. You begin an Outsider and you climb, but you cannot climb alone: every step up is paid for by the seals of your peers, and a peer who seals to you becomes your sworn vassal. Win their loyalty by the sword through War ’n Taxes vassalization wars, take a faction’s claims with Easy Factions to earn the crown, and watch your county, duchy and kingdom drawn on the map. The mod owns the progression and the relationships; the wars and the colonies come from the ecosystem it sits on.

How it fits together: MineColonies gives the colonies and the land. War ’n Taxes runs the vassalization wars whose outcomes become your seals. Easy Factions backs the road to King and draws faction borders. Antique Atlas 4 is the map your realm is painted onto. War ’n Nobility is the title and the loyalty graph that ties them together.

The Ladder

Outsider › Artisan › Knight › Baron › Count › Duke › King › Emperor

Each tier is earned a different way, so the lower rungs are granted by the world and the upper rungs are won from other players:

  • Outsider › Artisan › Knight › Baron are granted. An admin, an NPC, or a quest awards them (joining the realm, taking up a trade, being knighted after a boss, being made a Baron). You cannot self-promote through these; the server decides when you have earned them.
  • Baron › Count needs 2 seals from fellow Barons.
  • Count › Duke needs 2 seals from fellow Counts.
  • Duke › King is the Crown gate, the hardest step in the mod: 2 seals from fellow Dukes, and you must lead an Easy Factions faction holding at least the claim threshold (50 chunks by default), and you must craft and consume a Crown. See "The Crown" below.
  • King › Emperor is the throne. The first King ever crowned is automatically raised to Emperor (the empire was an empty throne waiting for its first claimant). After that, the crown only changes hands by usurpation.

The whole ladder is config-driven (names, requirements, and the how-to-advance text of every rank can be edited), so a server can reshape the titles to its own setting.

Seals & Vassalage

A seal is one peer of your own rank pledging their support to you. It is the currency of every ascension from Count upward.

  • You hold one outgoing pledge at a time: you seal to a single peer, and you can withdraw it while it is voluntary.
  • When that peer gathers enough seals and ascends, every one of their sealers becomes a sworn vassal, and their collected vassals become a County, a Duchy, or a Kingdom under them.
  • Seals are rank-enforced: you can only seal to someone of your own current rank, so a realm is built rung by rung, not skipped.
  • A forced seal is won by the sword, not given. It cannot be withdrawn, and it converts to a vassal bond on the victor’s ascension just like a voluntary one. This is how war feeds the ladder (below).

Vassal sentence (anti-cheese): a fresh vassal is bound for a set window (7 days by default, or pinned to the war’s own subjugation period) and cannot simply walk away; a liege or an admin can release them early. Losing vassals does not strip your title: once a Duke, the de jure rank sticks even if your duchy thins out.

Winning Vassals: War ’n Taxes

War ’n Nobility does not run the wars itself; it consumes the outcome of War ’n Taxes. When you win a vassalization war against a same-rank peer, that victory is turned into a forced seal automatically, your enemy becomes your vassal for the duration the war dictates.

  • A bridge polls the colonies a few times a minute and mirrors War ’n Taxes’ vassal state into nobility seals, so you never touch a command: win the war, gain the vassal.
  • It also reads War ’n Taxes’ own Diplomacy screen (Colony Tax Management › Diplomacy: Overlords / Vassals). Vassalize a peer directly there and the seal appears; set them back to Independent and the seal releases.
  • Only same-rank peer vassalizations become a legal ladder seal; a cross-rank subjugation is recorded but does not advance your title (you climb among equals).

Note: War ’n Taxes requires FTB Teams at runtime to run its wars, so any server using this integration needs FTB Teams installed alongside it.

The Crown: Becoming King

A Duke does not simply seal his way to a throne. Three things must all be true at once:

  • 2 Duke seals, the support of your fellow Dukes, as with every prior step.
  • A faction’s land. You must lead an Easy Factions faction that holds at least the claim threshold (50 claimed chunks by default). Crowns rest on territory, not just titles.
  • The Crown itself, crafted and consumed at the coronation.

The Crown & its Shard crafted from a rare drop

The Shard from the Crown of Unification has no recipe; it is awarded by admins or dropped by the bosses of the POIs & Raid Bosses mod, so the road to a crown runs through a real challenge. Combine the Shard with gold to forge the Crown, which is consumed when you are made King. If Easy Factions is absent, the faction half of the gate is skipped so the mod still works standalone, but on a full server the faction claim is part of the price.

The Emperor & Usurpation

There is one throne. The first King to ever rise takes it unopposed; after that, the only way to the imperial crown is to take it. A challenger must satisfy all three conditions:

  • Seize every Duke the Emperor holds, that is, vassalize all of the Dukes who answer directly to the sitting Emperor (the mod tracks this for you).
  • Hold the throne claim, an admin/war flag marking the seat of power as yours.
  • Win the war, the decisive-victory flag.

With all three met, the usurpation succeeds: the crown passes, and the old Emperor falls back to King. A live checklist shows a challenger exactly which of the three conditions they have, and which they still lack.

The Chancery Table

The Chancery Table (a lectern bearing a book) is your seat of governance. Right-click it to open the Chancery, with three tabs:

  • Court: your current standing and what it takes to rise; advance when you are eligible; pledge a seal to a same-rank peer; and, as a Count or higher, name your county, duchy or realm.
  • Settings: the Identify as Man / Woman toggle, which switches your title between its masculine and feminine forms everywhere.
  • Special Titles: reserved for bespoke honours, a place for future custom titles.

Solo-testing note: the seal buttons need other same-rank players online to pledge to, so the seal flow is a multiplayer feature.

Titles, Gender & Domains

  • Your title on your name: a gold [Title] prefix rides in front of your name in chat and on the tab list. (Floating nameplates above the head stay plain.)
  • Feminine titles: every rank has a feminine form, Dame, Baroness, Countess, Duchess, Queen, Empress, chosen by the Identify-as toggle (or the /nobility gender command) and used consistently across chat, tab and death messages.
  • Name your domain: a Count and above can name their county, duchy or realm; the name is stored and feeds the borders drawn on the map.

The Domain Map

Open the Antique Atlas 4 map and your political world is drawn over the terrain. Toggle buttons on the map switch five overlays on and off:

  • Colonies, the raw MineColonies claims.
  • Counties, Duchies, Kingdoms, the claims aggregated up your vassal tree, so a kingdom’s border is the sum of all the land its barons and counts hold, drawn as coloured pencil-edge borders with a name label at the heart of each.
  • Factions, Easy Factions claims as their own coloured layer, each faction tinted its own colour and labelled.

The map overlay is client-rendered and needs MineColonies and Antique Atlas present; without them, the rest of the mod still runs.

Commands

Administrative commands (operator / permission level 2). v1 addresses online players.

CommandWhat it does
/nobility promote / demote <player>Move a player one rank up or down the ladder
/nobility setrank <player> <rank>Force any rank, ignoring requirements (accepts the masculine or feminine name)
/nobility gender <player> male|femaleSet which title form a player uses
/nobility domain <player> <name>|clearName or clear a player’s county / duchy / realm
/nobility directive <rank> <text>|clearOverride a rank’s how-to-advance text, live and persisted
/nobility seal / unsealPlace or remove a peer seal by hand
/nobility subjugate <aggressor> <defender>The war-outcome hook: a forced seal (what War ’n Taxes calls)
/nobility vassal release|leaveA liege frees a vassal, or a vassal departs once the sentence ends
/nobility emperor throne|war|usurp|status <challenger>Set the throne/war flags, attempt a usurpation, or read the 3-point checklist
/nobility setscore <player> / infoAdjust the dormant score field; print a player’s nobility record
